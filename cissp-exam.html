How the CAT engine works

  • Every question is tagged with a difficulty (easy / medium / hard) and a domain.
  • Your ability estimate (θ) updates after each answer using a logistic (Rasch/IRT-style) model.
  • The next question is drawn near your current ability — miss one, it eases off; nail it, it pushes harder.
  • When the estimate's standard error gets small enough, the exam can stop early — just like the real CISSP CAT (100–150 items).

⚠️ These are original practice questions written in CISSP style across the official (ISC)² CBK domains. They are not real, leaked, or copyrighted exam items — the actual exam pool is confidential. Use this to drill concepts, not to memorize answers.
