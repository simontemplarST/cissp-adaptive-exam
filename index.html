How the CAT engine works

  • Every item is tagged with a difficulty and one of the 8 (ISC)² domains.
  • Your ability estimate (θ) updates after each answer using a logistic (Rasch/IRT) model.
  • The next item is drawn near your current ability and weighted toward the real exam's domain mix — miss one, it eases off; nail it, it pushes harder.
  • When the estimate's standard error is small enough, the exam can stop early — like the real CISSP CAT (100–150 items).

⚠️ These are original, generated practice questions built from verified fact tables across the (ISC)² CBK domains — not real, leaked, or copyrighted exam items (the actual pool is confidential, and using leaked pools can void a certification). Concepts are drawn from public references (NIST, OWASP, ISC², Wikipedia). Use this to drill understanding, not to memorize answers.
